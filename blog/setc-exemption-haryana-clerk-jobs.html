SETC Exemption Explained: How CCA Lets You Skip the Computer Test for Haryana Clerk Jobs

Government Jobs Updated 25 June 2026 8 min read

If you are preparing for a Haryana Government Clerk post, you have probably heard that a recognized computer course can save you from sitting one part of the selection process. That part is the SETC — and understanding it properly can save you time, money, and a lot of confusion. Here is the plain-language version.

What is the SETC?

SETC stands for State Eligibility Test for Computers. It is a computer-proficiency check used in Haryana to confirm that a candidate actually has the basic computer skills a clerical or office role needs — things like word processing, spreadsheets, and general computer operation. For several posts, clearing this requirement is part of being eligible for, or completing, the recruitment process.

In practice, candidates satisfy the computer-skill requirement in one of two ways: by qualifying the test itself, or by holding a computer certificate that the government recognizes as equivalent — which leads to an exemption from the written portion.

Where the CCA certificate comes in

Hartron's CCA (Course in Computer Application) is an NCVET-approved, NSQF-aligned certificate. Because it is a government-recognized qualification rather than a private certificate, it is recognized for exemption from the SETC written test for the Clerk Post. In other words, the certificate you earn after completing CCA can let you skip the written computer test instead of appearing for it separately.

This is the single biggest reason CCA is so popular with students targeting government jobs — and it is something no purely private institute's certificate can offer.

The advantage is not just "a certificate." It is a government-recognized certificate that is accepted in place of a government test. That distinction is what matters on a recruitment form.

Why "government-recognized" is the key phrase

There are many computer courses advertised across Hisar and Haryana. Most are private certifications. They may teach useful skills, but they do not carry weight in a government recruitment process. The exemption is tied to recognition — specifically NCVET approval and NSQF alignment — not to how long or how detailed the course looks in a brochure.

So when you compare courses, the question is not only "what will I learn?" It is also "will this certificate be accepted where I want to use it?" For Clerk-post aspirants, that answer needs to be yes.

What CCA actually covers

CCA at Hartron Skill Centre Hisar runs for 52 weeks and is built around real, job-relevant skills, not just exam theory. The syllabus includes:

  • Computer fundamentals and the Windows operating system
  • Word processing, spreadsheets, and digital presentations
  • Typing skills and internet basics
  • Introductory programming, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
  • An on-the-job training (OJT) project and viva, plus employability skills

That mix is intentional: it satisfies the computer-proficiency requirement and also leaves you genuinely capable in an office, not just certified on paper.

One honest caution before you rely on it

Government recruitment rules are updated from cycle to cycle. The names of tests, the list of accepted certificates, and the exact wording of requirements can change. So treat any computer certificate — including CCA — as one component of your eligibility, and always read the official recruitment notification (for example, from the Haryana Staff Selection Commission, HSSC) for the specific post and year you are applying in.

Do this before enrolling for a job-specific reason: check the latest official notification for the post you want, confirm which computer certificates it accepts, and keep a copy. If anything is unclear, ask us — we'll tell you honestly whether CCA fits your goal or whether another course suits you better.
